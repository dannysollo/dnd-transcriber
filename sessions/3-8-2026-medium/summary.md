# Session Summary — March 8, 2026

## Aziah Farewells & Equipment Reveals

The session opens in Aziah as the party prepares to depart for Fensworth. Kali pays a final visit to Del, who has been returned to her own body following Mikael's death — shaken but holding together, in part thanks to Kali's speech last session. Kali warns her to stay vigilant around Binah, who is likely to continue hunting for Sephirot cords, though an immediate attack seems unlikely after her failed attempt. Del takes it in stride. The party then runs through a shopping montage: Kali debuts her new legendary breastplate **Death's Eternal March**, a black-and-gold asymmetrical armored coat with resistance-shifting abilities, legendary resistance, and an on-bloodied damage bonus — the Nephilim robes officially retired. Aella reveals **Serpent Scale Armor** bringing her to 23 AC, and summons her new Eldritch Cannon: a miniature angel she calls the **Angel of Death**, which stuns enemies in a 20-foot radius before detonating. Kali does not love the theming.

## Fensworth: Aella's Hometown

The party travels to Fensworth, a struggling mining town where three miners were recently hospitalized after an incident in the mines. At the train station, Aella reunites with her younger brother **Nico**, who's been holding things together at home while their parents deal with tight food and money. Over the walk back, Aella catches him up on the broad strokes — Kolth, the Institute's destruction, getting knighted by the Queen, killing a couple of Sephirot. Nico, notably, reveals two things: first, he's been developing magical abilities over the last two months, learning prestidigitation in secret; second, he's been having recurring dreams of a hazy woman whose face he can't remember — a detail that immediately unsettles both Aella and Kali. He also shows off a necklace he found cleaning out the attic: a pocket watch gear piece inscribed with the name **Alexander Nouveau** — their grandfather.

## The Mines

Investigating the mine situation, the party learns the flooding isn't just stormwater from above — the Tehom has contaminated the town's water table, seeping up through the mine floor from below. The miners' accident appears to have been a freak ceiling collapse that exposed them directly to a Tehom pocket, not a deliberate ritual. Belle attempts to negotiate with a canary for passage into the mines (spectacularly fails), and Kali ends up buying two canaries from the gouging general store shopkeeper for eight gold — and gets firmly lectured about city-slicker behavior. Kali takes it on the chin and apologizes.

The party performs the anti-Tehom ritual powered by **Vixeena's CSF** as catalyst, causing the Tehom to rapidly recede from the mine. However, the soil remains damp and the ritual shows signs of deteriorating, indicating the fix is temporary. They chain the **Mer-power ritual** to the anti-Tehom ritual to make it self-sustaining. Belle confirms the Tehom appears cleared from the mine, but notes the underlying water table issue remains unresolved.

## Cliffhanger

A violent storm rolls in as the party shelters at Aella's family home. The windows are boarded up; the whole town waits tensely as thunder and an ominous cracking sound — like the earth splitting — rolls through. When the storm starts to ease, Nico goes to remove the window boards and takes one step down toward the basement. His foot lands in inky black Tehom. The session ends there.

---

*Key relationships shifted: Nico is now aware of the party's full story; the Tehom problem is deeper than a mine accident. Fensworth's water table is infected, and whatever cleared the mines is now inside Aella's house.*
